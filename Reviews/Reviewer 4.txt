The paper proposes a pipeline for classification of curves, applied to curves ensemble extracted from EBSD (electron microscopy imaging).
The pipeline is, to my understanding as follow:
- Embed curves as landmarks in Rnxd
- Use the Stiefel manifold formalism to compute mean/PCA curves
- Use the distance (MMD discrepancy test) to mean curve as the decision proxy
Strengths
I do think that the area of shape representation using mathematical tools is valuable and therefore I think what the paper proposes would benefit to the community
Weaknesses
Before starting on weaknesses, here's a general comment: I feel that the paper tends to overcomplicate concepts and mix them in a unpractical way. I had a very hard time reading it while the concepts presented stay relatively simple for the aware reader. Some parts feel completely useless or disordered. For example, on page 4, the presentation of the curve representation takes a long paragraph but could be described in a simple sentence. The notation paragraph contains barely any notation used (the curve space C_d is never defined). A whole paragraph on landmark normalization with a lot of discussion is presented, while the choice seems arbitrary. Also, authors never provide visualisations of what they are doing which require the reader to draw himself some of the concepts to understand them. Overall, I feel that the presentation is the main weakness of the paper which has 20 pages that could be easily reduce to 10 pages of text with illustrations when needed.
- The problem that the paper is trying to solve is not easy to understand. I believe it is to group curves by similarity but then why not using clustering based on the proposed approach
- The choice of evaluation, limited to EBSD feels restricted, while the paper is written in a very generic way. This makes it hard to read for EBSD practicioners as well as for Math Imaging people. An evaluation on similar datasets as in [1] would be great.
- An algorithm presenting the pipeline would be very helpful.
- The author claims a lot in the introduction the "interpretability" of the method, but it's not used otherwise. Learned shape features could produce the same results content. Moreover, none of the AI methods the paper target is (1) cited, (2) compared to.
References
Some references on the topic are ignored or wrong:
[1] Kaltenmark, I., Charlier, B., & Charon, N. (2017). A general framework for curve and surface comparison and registration with oriented varifolds. should probably in place of [38] (page 7 cited as varifolds)
[2] Pierson, E., Daoudi, M., & Arguillere, S. (2022, October). 3d shape sequence of human comparison and classification using current and varifolds. Represents human shapes as landmarks in RHKS - close to some concepts presented here.
[3] Bauer, M., Bruveris, M., Harms, P., & Møller-Andersen, J. (2017). A numerical framework for Sobolev metrics on the space of curves. Similar to [11] but on curves
[4] Hartman, E., & Pierson, E. (2023). Varigrad: A novel feature vector architecture for geometric deep learning on unregistered data. A learned feature vector for curves
[5] Hartman, E., Sukurdeep, Y., Charon, N., Klassen, E., & Bauer, M. (2021). Supervised deep learning of elastic SRV distances on the shape space of curves. Similar learning work.

Conclusion.
I tend towards rejection: the paper is far from being ready for publications, with mainly (1) missing experiments, comparison and quantification of the method success (2) bad quality presentation.
